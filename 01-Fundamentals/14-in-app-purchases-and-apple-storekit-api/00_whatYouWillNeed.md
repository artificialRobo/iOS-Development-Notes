# Notes: In-App Purchases (IAP)

## What are In-App Purchases?

* In-App Purchases (IAPs) allow developers to sell **virtual/digital products** within an app.
* Examples:

  * Gems
  * Coins
  * Energy
  * Premium features
  * Ad removal
* They are **not physical products**.

### Apple's Revenue Share

* Apple takes **30%** of all in-app purchase revenue.

  * Example:

    * $1 purchase → Apple keeps $0.30.
    * £1 purchase → Apple keeps £0.30.
* **All virtual products sold through the App Store must use Apple's IAP system.**

---

## Why Use In-App Purchases?

* Makes it easier for users to download a **free app**.
* Users can try the app before paying.
* Developers can monetize by:

  * Unlocking premium features
  * Removing ads
  * Selling extra content
* Many of the **top-grossing apps** are free but earn money through IAPs.

---

## Recommended Strategy (80/20 Rule)

* Give users about **80% of the app for free**.
* Lock the remaining **20% behind a paywall**.
* Premium content may include:

  * Extra features
  * Additional content
  * Ad-free experience

This approach increases user trust before asking them to pay.

---

## Example

The course uses a **Motivational Quotes App**:

* Users receive **6 free quotes**.
* Additional quotes are locked.
* Users buy an in-app purchase to unlock all quotes.
* After successful payment:

  * Premium content becomes available.

---

## Requirements Before Implementing IAP

You need:

1. **Apple Developer Program membership**

   * Costs **$99/year**.
   * Required to create and test IAPs.

2. **A physical iPhone**

   * IAPs **do not work on the iOS Simulator**.
   * Testing must be done on a real device.

## Key Takeaways

* In-App Purchases are for **digital goods only**.
* Apple processes all IAP payments and keeps **30%** of the revenue.
* Offering a free app with premium upgrades is an effective monetization strategy.
* Always test IAPs on a **real iPhone** with an **Apple Developer account**.
* A common approach is to provide most functionality for free and charge for premium features.
