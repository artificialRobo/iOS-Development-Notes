# Notes: Native Apps vs Cross-Platform Apps

## Types of Mobile Apps

* **Native Apps**

  * Built specifically for one platform.
  * **Android:** Java (or Kotlin) with the Android SDK.
  * **iOS:** Swift or Objective-C using Xcode.
* **Cross-Platform (Hybrid) Apps**

  * Built using frameworks like **Appcelerator, Xamarin, PhoneGap,** or **React Native**.
  * One codebase can be deployed to both Android and iOS.

### Advantages of Cross-Platform Apps

* Saves development time and cost.
* Single app can run on multiple platforms.

### Disadvantages of Cross-Platform Apps

* Often provides an **inferior user experience (UX)** compared to native apps.
* May not follow platform-specific design conventions.

  * **Android:** Commonly uses hamburger/menu drawers.
  * **iOS:** Typically uses a tab bar.
* Apps may not feel truly "native" to users.

---

## Why Learn Native App Development?

* Better performance and user experience.
* Easier to create apps that match platform expectations.
* Both **Google Play Store** and **Apple App Store** tend to **feature native apps** more often than cross-platform apps.
* Native apps may have an advantage in app marketing and promotion.

---

## React Native

* A modern and promising cross-platform framework.
* Worth learning and exploring.
* However, from a marketing perspective, native apps are generally easier to promote to Apple and Google.

---

## Key Takeaway

* **Native apps** offer the best user experience and have a higher chance of being featured by app stores.
* **Cross-platform apps** save time and money but may compromise on UX and store visibility.
* **React Native** is a strong cross-platform option, but native development remains highly valuable.
