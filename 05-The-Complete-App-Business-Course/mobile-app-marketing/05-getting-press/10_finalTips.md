## Notes: Best Practices for Pitching Journalists

### 1. Learn How Journalists Think

* Read advice from experienced journalists to improve your media pitches.
* A recommended resource is a blog post by **Mike Butcher** (Editor-at-Large at **TechCrunch**).
* Link for the blog - https://mikebutcher.me/2015/07/01/the-press-release-is-dead/
* The post explains:

  * How journalists prefer to be approached.
  * Common mistakes startups make when pitching.
  * A useful **pitch checklist** to review before contacting journalists.

**Key Tip:** Follow established best practices instead of sending generic or poorly prepared pitches.

---

### 2. Recommended Book

**Book:** *Trust Me, I'm Lying* by **Ryan Holiday**

Why it's recommended:

* Explains how **modern journalism** and online media operate.
* Covers the role of **click-driven journalism** and how stories spread across media.

### Key Ideas from the Book

* News often spreads through a chain:

  1. A story appears on a **small blog**.
  2. Larger blogs pick it up.
  3. Major newspapers and media outlets notice it.
  4. The story reaches **national newspapers** and **TV networks**.
* Ryan Holiday shares practical (and sometimes unconventional) strategies for generating media attention.

---

### Key Takeaways

* Understand **how journalists think** before pitching.
* Use **Mike Butcher's pitch checklist** to improve your outreach.
* Read **Trust Me, I'm Lying** to learn how modern media and press coverage work.
* Knowing how news spreads can help you gain more effective press coverage for your startup or app.
