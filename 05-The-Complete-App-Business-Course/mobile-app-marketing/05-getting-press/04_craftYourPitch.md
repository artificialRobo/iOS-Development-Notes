# Notes: How to Create an Effective Elevator Pitch

## Key Idea

* Use a **simple, clear structure** so anyone can quickly understand what your company or product does.
* Avoid confusing jargon or long, vague descriptions.

### Common Mistake

* Many startups describe themselves with buzzwords (e.g., "We are data integration social analytics company which helps you connect with your ...") without clearly explaining their purpose.
* If people can't understand what you do after hearing your pitch, it needs to be simplified.

### Recommended Structure

Use this formula:

> **My Company, [Company Name], is developing [product/service] to help [target customer] achieve [main benefit / problem solution] [with secret sause].**

**Example:**

* *SpaceX is developing reusable rockets to help space agencies reduce the costs of rocket launches.*

This format clearly explains:

1. **Who you are**
2. **What you're building**
3. **Who it's for**
4. **Why it matters**

---

## Elevator Pitch Tips

* Keep it **20–30 seconds** long.
* Get to the point quickly and don't waste time on unnecessary details.
* Focus on clarity rather than impressive-sounding language.

### Where to Use It

A strong elevator pitch is useful for:

* Pitching investors
* Talking to journalists
* Explaining your business to others
* Pitching customers
* Selling to B2B clients

### Action Item

* Write your own pitch using the recommended structure.
* Practice saying it until it sounds natural and concise.
* Clear, simple pitches generally receive better responses.
