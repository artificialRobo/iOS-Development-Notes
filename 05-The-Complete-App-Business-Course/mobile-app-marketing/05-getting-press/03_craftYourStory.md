# Notes: Crafting a Story for Journalists

## Why storytelling matters

* Journalists are unlikely to cover a company just because it has a new app or product.
* A successful pitch needs a **compelling story**, not just facts about the business.

## Think like a journalist

* Journalists are under pressure to produce articles that get:

  * Clicks
  * Shares
  * Likes
  * Views
* Make their job easier by providing everything they need:

  * High-quality images
  * Founder photos
  * Media assets
  * Any materials they can quickly use in their article

### Create an interesting angle

* Ask yourself:

  * Why would readers care?
  * What makes this unique, funny, surprising, or future-focused?
* Unknown companies can't rely on their brand name like Apple or Google can.
* A plain announcement ("We launched a new app") is usually not newsworthy.

---

## What makes a story newsworthy?

Good story angles often include:

* Seasonal relevance (e.g., holidays)
* Humor
* Curiosity or surprise
* A glimpse into the future
* Something unusual or unexpected
* High shareability

### Example: Christmas jumper campaign

* A company sold **humping reindeer Christmas jumpers**.
* Their story used:

  * **Seasonality** (Christmas shopping period)
  * **Clickbait appeal** (a funny headline about a grandmother designing the jumper)
* The story was first picked up by smaller publications and later by larger outlets like the Daily Mail.
* This generated:

  * Backlinks
  * Brand exposure
  * Increased sales

### Start small

* Pitch smaller blogs and local publications first.
* If the story is engaging, larger media outlets may pick it up through syndication.

### Important lesson

* The example's backstory was fabricated, but the key takeaway is **not** to invent false stories.
* Instead, identify a **truthful and interesting angle** that makes journalists and readers care.

---

## Key takeaway

**Craft the story before writing the pitch.** A strong, engaging angle dramatically increases the chances of media coverage, especially for companies that are not yet well known.
