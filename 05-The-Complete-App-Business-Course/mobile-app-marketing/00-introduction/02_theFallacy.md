# The Fallacy: Build It and They Will Come

## 1. The "Build It and They Will Come" Myth

* Simply creating a great app **does not guarantee success**.
* Many founders spend too much time perfecting their product without checking if people actually want it.
* Unvalidated ideas often lead to:

  * Wasted development time
  * Little or no user interest after launch
  * Unsustainable businesses

**Key takeaway:** Validate your idea before investing heavily in development.

---

## 2. Lean Startup & MVP

* Recommended book: **Lean Startup** by **Eric Ries**.
* Core principle: Build a **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)**.
* Launch early with basic functionality.
* Collect user feedback.
* Continuously improve the product based on real customer needs.

**Key takeaway:** Let users shape the product instead of relying only on your assumptions.

---

## 3. Always Validate Your Idea

* Validate your concept at every stage.
* Focus on solving real user problems.
* Even a well-designed product will fail if there is no market demand.

---

## 4. What Are "App Zombies"?

* **App Zombies** are apps ranked **below the 25th result** in the App Store.
* They are practically invisible because:

  * Most users don't scroll that far.
  * Similar to websites that rank beyond Google's first few pages.

**Result:** Very few downloads and little visibility.

---

## 5. App Store Saturation

* Zombie rates increased dramatically:

  * **2013:** ~57–58%
  * **2015:** ~90–91%
* This shows that app stores have become highly competitive.

**Implication:** Getting noticed is much harder than it used to be.

---

## 6. Importance of App Marketing

* Many independent developers do **no marketing**.
* Publishing an app and hoping for downloads is no longer effective.
* Success requires actively promoting your app.

Marketing helps you:

* Increase visibility
* Improve downloads
* Generate revenue
* Stay out of the "app zombie" category

---

## 7. Competition Varies by Category

Some categories are easier to rank in than others.

* **Lower competition:** Weather apps
* **Higher competition:** Entertainment apps
* **Very high competition:** Arcade and gaming apps

**Key takeaway:** The more competitive the category, the stronger your marketing strategy must be.

---

## 8. The New Reality of App Stores

Previously:

* Publish the app.
* Wait for users to find it.

Now:

* Publish the app.
* Actively promote it.
* Tell people why it's valuable.
* Continuously market it to attract users.

## Key Takeaways

* Don't rely on the "build it and they will come" mindset.
* Validate your idea before building.
* Start with an MVP and improve through user feedback.
* App stores are crowded, making discovery difficult.
* Marketing is essential for visibility and growth.
* Competitive app categories require even stronger marketing efforts.
* Successful apps combine **a valuable product with effective marketing**.
