# Notes: Launch Strategy & Finding the Right Audience

## Piggybacking on Existing Audiences

* Platforms like **Product Hunt**, **BetaList**, and **Hacker News** help startups gain traction by leveraging existing audiences.
* These platforms **complement**, but do not replace:

  * Building an email list
  * Creating inbound traffic through content and marketing

---

## Launches Are Not One-Time Events

* A failed launch is **not the end**.
* Treat launches as opportunities to gain growth, not as a single fixed date.
* Continue launching whenever you have a chance to attract new users or publicity.

### Redefining "Launch Day"

* A launch day is any significant moment that creates noticeable growth.
* Examples of launch events:

  * Being featured on Product Hunt
  * Media coverage (e.g., newspapers)
  * TV appearances
  * Other major publicity opportunities

### Example: Double App

The app treated multiple growth events as separate launch days:

1. **Product Hunt** feature
2. Feature in the **Daily Mail**
3. Appearance on **Dragon's Den** (UK version of *Shark Tank*)

Each event brought a new wave of users and publicity.

---

## Product Hunt vs. Television Exposure

* According to the founder of the Double app:

  * **Product Hunt generated more users, downloads, press, and attention than appearing on Dragon's Den.**
* Lesson: The platform where your audience already exists often matters more than the size of the platform.

### Know Your Target Audience

* Focus marketing efforts where your ideal users spend their time.
* A large audience is only valuable if it matches your target market.
* Example:

  * A serious newspaper may have many readers, but it won't be effective if your product targets teenagers.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Use audience-sharing platforms to accelerate growth.
* Keep building your own audience through email lists and inbound marketing.
* Don't view launch day as a one-time event—launch repeatedly whenever new opportunities arise.
* Measure launches by **growth**, not by a calendar date.
* Always choose promotional channels that align with your target audience.
