# Notes: How to Create Professional Mockups

## What are Mockups?

* **Mockups** are **high-fidelity (high-fi)** representations of a design.
* They should look almost identical to the final product, like real screenshots of the app.
* Mockups can be:

  * Displayed as individual screens.
  * Shown inside a phone or device to simulate real-world use.

---

## Design Tools for Mockups

### 1. Photoshop + Illustrator

**Advantages**

* Very powerful and comprehensive.
* Can handle almost any type of design work.

**Disadvantages**

* Steep learning curve.
* Complex features (layers, masks, pen tool, etc.).
* Expensive subscription through Adobe Creative Cloud.
* Better suited for experienced designers.

### 2. Sketch (Recommended)

**Why Sketch?**

* Designed specifically for UI/UX design.
* Easier to learn than Photoshop and Illustrator.
* Costs a one-time fee of **$99** (at the time of the lecture).
* Offers a **30-day free trial**.
* Supports **vector graphics**, allowing designs to scale without losing quality.
* Integrates well with prototyping tools.
* Can perform around **95%** of what Photoshop and Illustrator can for UI design.

**Best for**

* App interfaces
* Website mockups
* Icons and UI elements

### 3. Marvel

**Features**

* Browser-based design tool.
* Simple drag-and-drop interface.
* Used for both mockups and prototypes.
* Very beginner-friendly.
* Suitable for most app and website mockup needs.

### 4. UXPin

**Features**

* Includes pre-built UI component libraries (buttons, calendars, etc.).
* Drag-and-drop workflow.

**Drawbacks**

* Limited free tier.
* Monthly subscription pricing.
* Less cost-effective than learning Sketch.

---

## Vector vs. Raster Graphics

* **Vector graphics**

  * Scale to any size without losing quality.
  * Ideal for icons and UI elements.
  * Supported by Sketch and Illustrator.

* **Raster graphics**

  * Pixel-based images.
  * Lose quality when enlarged.
  * Primarily used in Photoshop.

---

## Key Takeaways

* After wireframes come **mockups**, which closely resemble the final product.
* **Sketch** is the recommended tool because it is affordable, easy to learn, and optimized for UI design.
* **Marvel** is an excellent beginner-friendly, browser-based alternative.
* **Photoshop and Illustrator** are powerful but expensive and more difficult to master.
* **UXPin** is useful but less attractive due to its pricing model.
* Learning **Sketch 3** is the next step in the course.
