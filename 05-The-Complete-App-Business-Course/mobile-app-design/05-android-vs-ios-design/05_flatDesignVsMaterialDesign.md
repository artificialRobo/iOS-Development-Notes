# Notes: Flat Design vs Material Design

## 1. Flat Design (iOS)

* Before **iOS 7**, Apple used **skeuomorphic design**:

  * Glassy, 3D icons
  * Shadows, gradients, and high contrast
* Since **iOS 7**, Apple adopted **Flat Design**, which features:

  * Minimal or no shadows
  * No gradients or 3D effects
  * Low contrast
  * A clean, simple appearance that resembles a **flat piece of glass**

---

## 2. Material Design (Android)

* Google introduced **Material Design**, moving away from its earlier flat design approach.
* Material Design is inspired by **real-world materials** like paper and cards.
* Features include:

  * Subtle shadows and depth
  * Layered elements
  * Clear visual hierarchy
  * Interactive elements that appear raised from the background

---

## 3. Comparison

| **Flat Design (iOS)**            | **Material Design (Android)**  |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Minimalistic appearance          | Uses subtle depth and layers   |
| No shadows or gradients          | Includes shadows and elevation |
| Looks like a flat sheet of glass | Looks like stacked paper/cards |
| Fewer visual cues                | Clearer interactive elements   |

---

## 4. User Experience (UX)

* Flat Design is visually clean but may make it harder for users to distinguish:

  * Buttons
  * Labels
  * Interactive elements
* Material Design provides subtle visual cues, making it easier for users to understand what can be interacted with.
* According to the speaker, Material Design offers a better user experience because it improves usability while maintaining a modern appearance.

---

## Key Takeaways

* **iOS:** Uses **Flat Design**—simple, minimal, and glass-like.
* **Android:** Uses **Material Design**—adds subtle depth and shadows inspired by real materials.
* Material Design improves usability by making interactive elements easier to recognize.
* Good UI design balances visual simplicity with clear interaction cues.
