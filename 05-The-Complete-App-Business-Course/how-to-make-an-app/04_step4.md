# Step 4: App Development

## From Planning to Development

Before development, you should have:

* A validated app idea
* A complete app design
* A monetization strategy

The next step is to **turn your idea into a working app through programming**.

---

## Ways to Develop an App

There are three main approaches, ranging from the most expensive to the least expensive.

---

### 1. Hire an App Development Agency (Most Expensive)

#### How it works

* Work directly with professional designers and developers.
* Receive guidance throughout the development process.
* Stay closely involved with the project.

#### Advantages

* High-quality expertise
* Professional project management
* Easier communication

#### Disadvantages

* Very expensive
* Typical cost:

  * **£80,000–£150,000 per app** (iOS or Android)

---

### 2. Hire Freelance Developers

Many developers work remotely through freelance platforms.

  <img src="assets/freelancer.png" alt="" width="auto">
</p>

#### Advantages

* Lower cost than agencies
* Access to global talent

#### Typical Cost

* Around **£30,000–£50,000** per app

#### Payment Methods

* **Hourly rate:** Pay for time worked.
* **Milestone payments:** Pay when specific stages are completed.

---

### Choosing Freelancers Carefully

Before hiring:

* Interview them multiple times (video calls if possible).
* Review previous projects.
* Speak with past clients.
* Verify their experience and reliability.

#### Lesson from a Case Study

A student hired an overseas development team that later claimed:

* Their computers had been seized.
* They demanded extra money to continue the project.

The project was abandoned because the team could no longer be trusted.

**Key lesson:** Always perform thorough background checks before hiring.

---

### 3. Build the App Yourself (Least Expensive)

Learning app development gives you the greatest control.

#### Benefits

* Lowest development cost
* Full customization
* No dependence on outside developers
* Valuable long-term skill

Even if you don't become a full-time developer, programming knowledge helps you:

* Understand technical discussions
* Estimate project costs
* Judge development timelines
* Communicate better with development teams

---

## Learning to Code

The speaker emphasizes that:

* Many beginners have successfully learned app development.
* No prior programming experience is required.
* Thousands of students have built and launched their own apps after learning.

Programming is presented as an achievable skill for anyone willing to learn.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Begin development only after validating the idea, designing the app, and planning monetization.
* Three main development options:

  1. **Agency** – highest quality but most expensive.
  2. **Freelancers** – lower cost but requires careful vetting.
  3. **Build it yourself** – cheapest, most flexible, and provides valuable skills.
* If hiring freelancers, always:

  * Interview them.
  * Check references.
  * Review previous work.
* Learning programming is a worthwhile investment, even if you later hire developers.

### Overall Takeaway

Choosing the right development approach depends on your **budget, technical skills, and desired level of control**. Building the app yourself is the most cost-effective and empowering option, while hiring professionals requires careful planning and due diligence.
